Course Elective Type Change

For all PG programs the maximum course credits to be allowed for conversion (Additional to regular and vice-versa) are 8 credits.

The following details in addition to your bio are required to be filled:

  • Tick as applicable: Elective to Additional/Additional to Elective.
  • Course Number, Course Name, No. of credits, Semester, Currently the course registered as (Core/Free Elective/ Additional), To be shown as (Core/Free Elective/Additional)
  • The Signatures of the Guide/Faculty Advisor and the DPGC along with their names and date are to be filled.

Students who received 'FR' grade in Electives and want to opt for Substitution of Elective Course have to fill the following details in addition to your Name, Roll.No and Department:

  • Substitution Elective Type (Core/ Liberal Arts Elective), Program and Year.
  • Course Substitution Details, which contains details of course completed with 'FR' Grade, Proposed Substitution Course Details (Course Number, Course Title, Credits, Semester)
  • The Signatures of the student, Guide/Faculty Advisor and the HoD along with their names and date are to be filled.

The form can be downloaded from the following link: Course-Substitution-Form

Majors/Minors

Double Major

  • There is no eligibilty requirement for registration in terms of credits or projects.
  • There is no eligibility requirement in terms of number of backlogs.
  • There is no CGPA criterion for registration.
  • Departments can set their own maximum number of students that can avail this option.
  • You can apply starting from 4th Semester.
  • In case the student from your department wants to pursue an option in your department, you can inform/seek approval from the Faculty advisor. Further, the advisor should inform the academic section.
  • If a student would like to withdraw from major , he/she can contact the Faculty adisor.
  • After enrolling into majors, if a student gets FS/FR grade in more than 3 credits his/her enrolment to the same will be terminated.
  • If a student wants to pursue more than one option such as major as well as minor, then he/she should be permitted on the recommendation of the Faculty Advisor. Minor and major cannot be done in the same department.
  • CGPA shall be displayed as B.Tech (parent dept) as well as B.Tech (second dept).

Minor

  • There is no eligibility requirement for registration in terms of number of credits or projects.
  • A student must have cleared all outstanding backlogs by the time of enrolment into Minors/Honors
  • There is no CGPA criterion for Minor. However, a CGPA criterion for Honors can be set by the respective Departments.
  • To avoid overloading, departments offering Minor/Honors can put an upper limit on the number of Minor/honor students they wish to take. The students have to understand that since the number of seats available for each of the program will always be limited, one has to compete for a place; he/she thus cannot ignore the basic CGPA.
  • A student can enroll for Minor in fifth semester only. There is no CGPA criterion for enrolling into a Minor. A student can enroll for Honors in fifth or sixth semester, depending on the policy of the department. The department can plan the Honors from fifth or sixth semester. There is no CGPA criterion for enrolling into Honors at institute level.
  • You can inform/seek approval from Faculty advisor in case the student from your department wants to pursue an option in another department.
  • You can inform/seek approval from DUGC Convener in case the student from another department wants to pursue an option in your department.
  • Faculty advisor is required to inform the acedemic office of the same.
  • If a student would like to withdraw from major, minor or honours, you can contact through the faculty advisor.
  • After enrolling into Minors/Honors, if a student gets FS/FR grade in more than 3 credits his/her enrolment to the same will be terminated.
  • If a student has done the required courses as free electives before, you can make up for the lost credits on a case by case basis on recommendation of DUGC convener.
  • Minor and major cannot be done in the same department. Student can enroll for both Minor & Honors or for 2 Minors.

Additional Details

  • In order to earn a minor, a student has to earn a minimum of 12 extra credits from a basket of courses prescribed for each minor stream.
  • In order to earn honors, a student has to earn a minimum of 12 extra credits out of which six credits from project work and at least six credits are from courses offered by the student’s major department. These can change as per department guidelines.
  • The final transcript will only show the basic CGPA corresponding to the minimum requirement for the degree. The Minors/Honors will be indicated by a separate CGPA. The additional courses taken will also find separate mention in the transcript.
  • If a student drops (or terminated) from the Minor/Honors program, they cannot convert the earned credits into free or core electives; they will remain extra. These additional courses will find mention in the transcript (but not in the degree certificate). In such cases, the student may choose between the actual grade or a “pass(P)” grade and also choose to omit the mention of the course as for the following: All the courses done under the dropped Minor/Honors will be shown in the transcript. None of the courses done under the dropped Minor/Honors will be shown in the transcript.

Scholarships

Institute Scholarships

Merit-cum-Means (MCM)

GEN, GEN-EWS / OBC category students can apply

  • Parental Income shall not exceed Rs. 4.5 Lakhs for any category.
  • Income Tax Return with Acknowledgement from IT Department has to be submitted as Income Proof of Parents (Father and Mother or Guardian if any)
  • The number of students to be awarded MCM scholarship will be limited to 25% of Class Strength.
  • The student should secure a SGPA / CGPA of 7.0 and above with no active backlogs (Fail Grade) in any type of courses (Including Additional courses).
  • The Shortlisted students are eligible for Pocket Money of Rs.1,000 per month.

SC/ST Scholarship

  • Students belonging to SC / ST category can apply.
  • Parental Income shall not exceed Rs. 4.5 Lakhs for any category.
  • Income Tax Return with Acknowledgement from IT Department has to be submitted as Income Proof of Parents (Father and Mother or Guardian if any)
  • There is no ceiling on the award of SC / ST scholarship subject to fulfilling the required criteria.
  • The student should secure a SGPA / CGPA of 7.0 and above with no active backlogs (Fail Grade) in any type of courses (Including Additional courses).
  • The Shortlisted students are eligible for Pocket Money of Rs.250 per month and are eligible for refund of License Fee and Dining Charges at actuals and as per the eligibility.
